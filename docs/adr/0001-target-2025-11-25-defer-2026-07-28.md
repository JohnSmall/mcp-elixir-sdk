## Context

At the start of the re-appraisal (MES-1), the MCP specification landscape had two live revisions:

- **2025-11-25** — the current stable/Final revision. The February build of `mcp_elixir_sdk` was
  written against it.
- **2026-07-28** — a release candidate (published 2026-05-21), described by the maintainers as the
  largest revision since launch: a stateless protocol core (removing the `initialize` handshake and
  `Mcp-Session-Id` session), an extensions framework, Tasks, MCP Apps, authorization hardening, and a
  formal deprecation policy.

"Fully standards compliant" was therefore ambiguous. Targeting the RC would mean rebuilding the
transport against a specification that could still change before its final publication, and before a
verified baseline of the existing code even existed. The immediate business need — surfaced by the
consumer EMFA — was a narrow, additive capability (the `handler_opts` request-identity seam), not a
protocol migration.

## Decision

**Target MCP revision 2025-11-25 only.** Defer any 2026-07-28 work to a future epic. All Sprint 0–2
work (the seam spec, implementation, acceptance tests, and the 1.1.0 release) is scoped to 2025-11-25
and treats 2026-07-28 observations as forward notes, not actionable work.

## Rationale

- **Don't build against a moving target.** The 2026-07-28 revision was a release candidate; committing
  a transport rewrite to it risked redoing the work if the RC shifted before final.
- **Verify before you migrate.** You cannot plan a foundational migration from an unverified baseline.
  The re-appraisal (MES-1) had to establish where the February build actually stood against the
  revision it was written for.
- **Ship consumer value quickly.** EMFA needed the `handler_opts` seam, an additive change orthogonal
  to spec revision. Coupling it to a multi-sprint migration would have delayed a small, needed fix.
- **The stateless core is a clean break, not an increment.** It removes the handshake and session the
  existing GenServer transport is built on — foundational, and deserving of its own deliberate
  decision rather than being bundled into feature work.

## Consequences

- The SDK ships and is maintained on 2025-11-25; `mcp_elixir_sdk` 1.1.0 was released against it
  (2026-07-13) and adopted by EMFA. **This part of the decision stands and is not superseded.**
- The SDK is knowingly one revision behind the 2026-07-28 line once that finalises.
- The seam's design binds identity at the `initialize` session — a binding point 2026-07-28 removes.
  This becomes the central migration problem (see ADR-002).
- 2026-07-28 items were parked as recurring forward notes across Sprint 2 artefacts.

## Alternatives considered

- **Target 2026-07-28 (the RC) directly.** Rejected: maximum rework, against an unstable target,
  before a verified baseline existed.
- **Phased: stabilise on 2025-11-25 now, migrate later.** This is effectively what was chosen — with
  the migration left as an explicitly deferred future epic rather than a scheduled sprint.

## Supersession

The **deferral** in this ADR is superseded by
[ADR-002](0002-adopt-2026-07-28-stateless-core-migration.md): the 2026-07-28 RC is now locked and a
consumer requirement drives adoption, so the migration moves from "deferred" to "scheduled." ADR-001's
decision to _ship 1.1.0 against 2025-11-25_ is unaffected and remains in force.
